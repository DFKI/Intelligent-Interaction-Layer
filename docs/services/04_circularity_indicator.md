# Circularity Indicator

Scores how circular a product is on a 0–1 index with an A–E grade, based on four
things — recycled content, recyclability, durability, and repairability — and
suggests how to improve.

## What you can ask

> What's the circularity score for 40% recycled content, 75% recyclable, durability 7/10, repairability 6/10?
>
> What's the circularity grade for a product with 20% recycled input, 60% recyclable, durability 5, repairability 4?
>
> How can I improve my product's circularity from grade D to B?

## What you get back

- A circularity index (0–1) and an A–E grade
- A score for each of the four dimensions
- How it compares to a reference value
- Specific suggestions to improve the weakest areas

## Good to know

- The four dimensions follow the EU **EN 4555x** material-efficiency standards
  (durability, repair, recyclability, recycled content).
- Express the numbers however feels natural — recycled content and
  recyclability as percentages ("75%") or fractions ("0.75"), and durability
  and repairability out of 10 or out of 100 — the service normalizes the scale.
- How the dimensions are combined (the weights and the reference value) are
  transparent, configurable assumptions — see
  [Methodology & Limitations](../methodology.md). This is not the Ellen MacArthur
  Material Circularity Indicator.
