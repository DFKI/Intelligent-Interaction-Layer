# Product Carbon Footprint

Estimates a product's carbon footprint (kg CO₂e) from its materials, weight, and
where it's made — with a breakdown by materials, manufacturing, and transport,
and a simple A–E rating.

## What you can ask

> Calculate the carbon footprint of a 2 kg laptop made in China that's 40% aluminum, 35% plastic, and 25% steel.
>
> What's the carbon footprint of a 3 kg steel chair made in Europe?
>
> Which material in my product drives most of the emissions?

## What you get back

- The total carbon footprint in kg CO₂e
- A breakdown by materials / manufacturing / transport
- An A–E carbon rating and how it compares to a typical product
- The potential saving from switching to recycled materials

## Good to know

- Just describe the materials, a rough weight, and the region — the assistant
  handles the rest, and will ask if something's missing.
- Give the material shares however feels natural — as percentages
  ("40% aluminum") or as fractions ("0.4") — the service reads either scale.
- The material emission factors come from published life-cycle datasets. The
  calculation method, the factors, and what's an assumption are documented in
  [Methodology & Limitations](../methodology.md).
